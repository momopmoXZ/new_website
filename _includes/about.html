What is Unibots?

A national challenge with a friendly starting line

Founded in 2022 by students from the University of Cambridge and King's College London, Unibots gives university teams a reason to design, build, test, and improve an autonomous robot together.

Each season introduces a new arena challenge with clear rules and many possible solutions. Teams compete head-to-head, creating an energetic spectator event while learning the realities of engineering: making decisions, solving problems, and adapting when things do not go to plan.

The competition journey

From first idea to finals weekend

Teams have time to develop their robot before meeting at regional events and the two-day finals. Regionals are opportunities to practise in the arena, learn from other teams, and improve before the main competition.

Design and build Form a team, interpret the challenge, and turn your approach into a working autonomous robot.
Test at regionals Try the arena, meet other teams, and take useful lessons back to your workshop.
Compete at finals Join a weekend of networking, talks, practice, and head-to-head competition.

Student-led and collaborative

Organised across universities

The fifth edition of Unibots is co-hosted by the University of Cambridge Robotics Society, Heriot-Watt University Robotics Society, and Queen Mary University of London Electronics Society. Together, we are building a stronger network between robotics communities across the UK.
